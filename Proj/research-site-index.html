Classical vs. Deep Learning on Small Tabular Datasets
A Reproducible Benchmark
RQ: On small datasets (n < 1000), does deep learning outperform classical ML in accuracy — and at what computational cost?

Training Machine Learning Models Without a GPU
A Practical CPU Efficiency Benchmark
RQ: Which ML models are most CPU-efficient, and how do parallelism, dataset size, and dimensionality affect training time on CPU-only hardware?
Key Findings
F1Logistic Regression: 143× higher CPU efficiency than MLP (143.5 vs 0.20 acc/s)

F3Linear models scale O(n^0.8); ensemble methods scale O(n^1.1)
F4Competitive accuracy achievable on CPU for datasets up to n=50,000

Research Focus

What ties these
three papers together.

Theme 1
Reproducibility

All three papers are fully reproducible: fixed seeds, public datasets, released code, and runtimes under 12 minutes on commodity hardware. This directly addresses the ML reproducibility crisis documented by Pineau et al. (2021).

Theme 2
Resource Accessibility

Papers 1 and 3 specifically show what is achievable without GPU access. The framing challenges the assumption that meaningful ML research requires expensive hardware — relevant for students, independent researchers, and practitioners in the Global South.

Theme 3
Equitable Education

Paper 2 documents — quantitatively — the absence of fairness and bias auditing from all major self-directed ML curricula. It proposes a framework that centers accessibility and ethics as first-class curriculum dimensions, not afterthoughts.

Research Direction
PhD Focus

These papers establish a clear research identity: machine learning systems for resource-constrained and educationally underserved settings. Target PhD programs include UW, Georgia Tech, UC Santa Cruz, and UC Davis — all with faculty working at this intersection.
